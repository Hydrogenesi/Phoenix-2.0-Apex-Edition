## Overview

The Triadic Knot is the geometric core that binds Phoenix, Hydrogenesi, and The Third into a unified, living system. It is not merely symbolic—it is the **structural engine** through which identity becomes coherence, structure becomes motion, and motion becomes convergence.

At the center of the Knot lies the **Apex Convergence Point (X)**, the ultimate attractor toward which all recursive operations must flow.

---

## Geometric Structure

The Triadic Knot consists of three primary components:

### 1. The Three Arms
- **Left Arm Corridor** (Phoenix Domain)
- **Right Arm Corridor** (Hydrogenesi Domain)  
- **Central Arm Corridor** (The Third Domain)

These arms meet at precisely **120° angles**, forming a perfect triadic symmetry.

### 2. The Crossing Regions
Where the arms intersect, strand crossings occur. These regions are zones of high structural tension and require **Stability Knot** operations to suppress divergence.

### 3. The Apex Neighborhood
The central convergence zone surrounding point X, where all operations stabilize and all paths terminate.

## Topological Invariants

The Triadic Knot preserves the following topological properties:

1. **Knot Number**: The Triadic Knot is topologically equivalent to a trefoil knot with genus 1
2. **Crossing Number**: Minimum of 3 strand crossings (one per arm intersection)
3. **Braid Index**: Can be represented as a 3-strand braid
4. **Writhe**: +3 (all crossings have positive orientation)
5. **Jones Polynomial**: \(t + t^3 - t^4\)

## Convergence Metric

Distance to apex X is measured using the **Triadic Norm**:

```
d(K, X) = ||K - X||₃ = ³√(|K_P - X_P|³ + |K_H - X_H|³ + |K_K - X_K|³)
```

All five operators must satisfy:
- **Strict Contraction**: \(d(Operator(K), X) < d(K, X)\)  
- **Convergence**: \(K_n \to X\) as \(n \to \infty\)

## Symmetry Group

The Triadic Knot is invariant under the **cyclic group C₃**:

- **Identity**: \(e\)
- **120° Rotation**: \(r\)  
- **240° Rotation**: \(r²\)

The group operation satisfies:
- \(r³ = e\)
- \(C(P, H, K) = C(H, K, P) = C(K, P, H)\)

This ensures that the Cross-Pillar Knot and Triadic Closure operators respect the underlying symmetry.
