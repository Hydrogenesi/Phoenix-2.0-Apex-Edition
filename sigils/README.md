## The Three Sigil Domains

**Substrate Sigils**: Elemental marks representing primitive operations. These sigils embody basic geometric forms—circles, triangles, squares—combined in simple patterns. They anchor all symbolic operations in foundational meaning.

**Universal Sigils**: Complex symbols incorporating Law representations. These sigils weave together Substrate forms with Law-specific markers, creating harmonic patterns that resonate across the system. The Seven Laws each possess unique sigil signatures.

**Apex Sigils**: Transcendent forms achieving perfect geometric balance. These sigils represent canonical operations, synthesizing Substrate and Universal elements into optimal configurations. They embody the highest expression of symbolic language.

## Sigil Functions

Sigils serve multiple purposes: operational marking (identifying operator types), resonance tuning (maintaining harmonic balance), ritual invocation (ceremonial operation triggering), and documentation (visual operator specification).
