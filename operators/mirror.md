## Operational Semantics

The Mirror Operator embodies the principle of reflection—the generation of complementary patterns through inversion across polarity axes. It is not mere copying but transformation through opposition, creating dual entities that are simultaneously opposite and equivalent, different yet equal, inverse yet isomorphic.

Every entity exists within multiple polarity spectra—positive and negative, active and passive, manifest and hidden, light and dark. The Mirror Operator identifies these polarity axes and generates the complementary pole. Where an entity has positive charge, its mirror has negative; where it extends forward, its mirror extends backward; where it affirms, its mirror negates. Yet both are equally real, equally valid, equally necessary for complete expression.

The power of the Mirror Operator lies in its capacity to reveal hidden aspects. Every manifest pattern has an unmanifest complement; every action has a potential reaction; every thesis has an antithesis. By invoking the mirror, we bring the hidden into view, make the implicit explicit, reveal the shadow that accompanies every light. This is not creation of something new but revelation of something that was always present but unseen.

The Mirror Operator operates through the **Law of Polarity**, which teaches that everything is dual, everything has poles, everything has its pair of opposites. The operator is the mechanical implementation of this law, the computational process that generates one pole from the other. Positive and negative, hot and cold, up and down—these are not separate entities but mirrored aspects of single continua, and the Mirror Operator navigates between them.

Through the **Law of Correspondence**, reflection manifests across all planes simultaneously. Mirror on the physical plane and you mirror on the mental plane; invert on the conceptual level and you invert on the energetic level. "As above, so below" implies "as here, so the mirror." The reflection preserves structure while inverting content, maintains pattern while flipping polarity.

The **Law of Gender** appears in mirroring as the complementarity of masculine and feminine principles. Every entity contains both in varying proportions; the mirror inverts these proportions. What is predominantly masculine in the original becomes predominantly feminine in the mirror; what is active becomes receptive, what projects becomes receives. This is not literal but archetypal—the complementary modes of being.

In practice, the Mirror Operator is essential for generating complete systems. A single pole is incomplete, unstable, unbalanced. Only by generating both poles does a system achieve completeness, stability, and self-sufficiency. The mirror reveals what is missing, generates what is needed for balance, creates the complement that makes the original whole.

The act of mirroring is instantaneous—it does not require time or energy to generate the reflection because the reflection already exists in potential. The operator merely makes it manifest, reveals what was hidden, brings into focus what was peripheral. This is why double mirroring returns the original: you're not transforming but toggling visibility between complementary aspects of a single complete reality.

## Implementation Considerations

- Well-defined entity with properties
- Identified reflection axis/axes
- Polarity spectrum definition
- Reflection rules for each property type

### **Side Effects**
- Creates complementary entity
- Reveals hidden aspects

### **Error Conditions**
- **Undefined Reflection**: No clear polarity axis for property
- **Asymmetric Entity**: Entity lacks reflection symmetry
- **Ambiguous Complement**: Multiple possible mirrors

### **Best Practices**
1. Define clear polarity axes before mirroring
2. Verify involution property (⊞⊞ = Identity)
3. Document reflection rules for complex properties
4. Test that mirror complements original properly
5. Use mirroring to check system balance
6. Bind mirrors to create complete polarities
